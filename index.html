US leads post-COVID reduction in gas flaring

By Kunle Falayi

The U.S., one of the top contributors to gas flaring among oil producing nations of the world, reduced its gas flaring by more than 3 billion cubic meters in 2021 after flaring almost 12 billion cubic meters of gas in the first COVID year 2020.

World Bank's 2022 Global Gas Flaring Data shows gas flaring levels spiked in 2019 to 17.29 billion. The oil and gas industry says the practice, which introduces pollutants like sulfur dioxide and generates greenhouse gases, is unavoidable.



Even though the data shows that Iran made the highest increase in post-COVID gas flaring levels, Russia maintains the lead as the highest contributor globally, burning over 25 billion m3 of gas in the atmosphere in 2021.
